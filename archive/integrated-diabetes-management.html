Integrated Lifestyle Management System

    CHALLENGE

"How might we prevent the progression of illness for early stage type 2 diabetes patients?"

  PROJECT

I developed an integrated lifestyle management system for early stage type 2 diabetes patients to understand the impact of food and workout on their glucose level. Using machine learning and image recognition, patients are able to view the predicted change in glucose level based on algorithms developed from data.

  RESEARCH

Early stage Type II Diabetes, also known as Prediabetes, is a high blood sugar condition that is likely to develop into type 2 diabetes. However, the progression can be reversed with lifestyle changes, weight loss and medication.

To understand the impact of this condition on day-to-day living, I interviewed a senior citizen, Elizabeth, with Prediabetes and mapped out her support system as well as her typical daily routine. In addition, I constructed a glucose metabolic system of a prediabetes patient's body based on secondary research and indicated the disturbances to the system.

  PROPOSED CONCEPT

The product includes an integrated wearable sensor that monitors glucose level and heart rate, an optimization model of machine learning algorithm to predict the change in glucose level, and a mobile app for patients to interact with the integrated system.
The interactions between a patient and the system define the key user tasks and then the user conceptual model of the mobile app is outlined as a result.

  KEY IDEAS IMPLEMENTATION

The following mockup screens showcased how the integrated system is helping patients to manage their lives through two key questions: 

"How will the food intake increase blood glucose level?"

"How is exercise helping to bring down glucose level?"

Primary Functions


The app allows the patient to see their current Glucose level, log their food and activities, view statistics, and input personal parameters.

Glucose Level Prediction


While logging food, patients will be able to view the glycemic index for different recipes. They can also scan food nutrition information while purchasing.

These food data, combining with the patient's individual profile data, will be passed into the machine learning prediction model and generate a unique prediction.

Correlation with Lifestyle


In daily statistics, patients will be able to view the relationship between glucose peak and mealtime. They can also see the predicted glucose level without exercise and the differences highlighted.

  KEY LEARNING

Through this project, I learned about the feedback loop and also how a mobile app is also part of a larger system. It is important that the system does more than monitoring and controlling, but also it optimizes itself and eventually reaches autonomy. The understanding of the overall system enables me to see how different touchpoints are needed for the user to be engaged. I really enjoy this approach to design interactions and I hope to take advantage of this approach for all my future projects.
