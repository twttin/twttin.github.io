Becoming Activists

- Empowering the next generation of leaders

Overview

The project started from a in-depth conversation with a leader in activism and art. It was fascinating to learn about their perspective on "truth and authenticity", and how they serve as an alternative way to mediate information in an increasingly polarized world today.

In working on this project, we learned from the activists and came up with ways that helps them to inspire the next generation through the society ideology structure.

Approach

We started the project with a detailed research plan that outlines various research methodology and a discussion guide. Key insights were generated from our research through remote synthesis sessions. We were then able to define the opportunity by framing the problem space. We then ended up with a few recommended design solutions that could be implemented as a response to the opportunity.

research goal

To learned the stories of how activists become who they are today, what they are determined to achieve now, and what they believe needs to be act upon in the future.

key research questions

How has the media portrayed you as a group?

In regards to activism, how do you understand truth and authenticity? What does it mean to your work?

What are the powers you’re fighting against? What are the dynamics of power in your activism space?

Who do you want to share the information with? Why do you think they might not have access to some of this information?

understanding the context

Key insights

Activists and media both choose to highlight information that supports their cause and underplay other information.

The empowering moment happens when one realizes one's ability to voice out and influence others.

People unconsciously operate within societal structures and ideologies.

Activists need to realize their personal truth, come to terms with their power and privilege in order to fight external perceptions and come together as a collective.

"How might we help activist leaders to inspire the next generation to act upon their personal truth?"
